Hi!  I'm a Postdoctoral Fellow in the statistics department at the University of Michigan advised by Jonathan Terhorst and studying the interplay between topological data analysis and evolutionary statistics.

In 2025, I received my Ph.D. in mathematics from the University of Oregon under the advisorship of Dev Sinha.
My dissertation, Multiscale 2-Mapper: Exploratory Data Analysis Guided by the First Betti Number, lies at the intersection of topological data analysis and computational geometry. My general research interests include applications of topology and geometry toward the computational and natural sciences, such as data analysis, population genomics and evolutionary statistics.
